A dual-axis Rubik's Cube rotates in slow vertical turns and random horizontal tilts, revealing grayscale emotional fragments with each pause. The stationary woman observes — her gesture echoing the original Shapes of Gray pose — while chiaroscuro reflections flicker across her face in a ritual of perception.

The Artist

Douglas Greedale

Douglas Greedale works at the intersection of graphite portraiture and digital animation — exploring the fragmented nature of human perception through geometric structures and dramatic chiaroscuro.

"Each turn of the cube reveals new emotional fragments — inscribing a ritual of perception and dramaturgical reflection."

His works place the observer as both subject and witness. Shapes of Grey and the pencil series extend this practice into motion and stillness simultaneously — a duality that defines his visual language.
